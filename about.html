My Story

Rooted in Compassion, Built on Experience

Lillya Nashanchik is a transition advisor who supports individuals and families through major life changes after 55, including downsizing, relocation, and complex next chapters. She brings a calm, structured approach that blends strategic clarity with hands-on guidance.

Lillya founded The Next Chapter Concierge after witnessing firsthand how disorienting and isolating major life transitions can be — both for the individuals experiencing them and for the family members trying to help. She recognized a gap: families needed more than a checklist. They needed a steady, trusted presence that could hold space for both the emotional weight and the practical complexity of change.

With a background in organization, family dynamics, and senior care coordination, Lillya brings a rare combination of empathy and structure to every engagement. She understands that behind every move is a person — with a lifetime of stories, relationships, and deeply held values — and she never loses sight of that.

"I've seen what happens when families try to navigate these transitions alone. It doesn't have to be that hard. With the right support and the right timing, a major life transition can become one of the most meaningful chapters of someone's life."

Lillya serves clients throughout the New York metro area and works with families nationally on complex transition projects.

Philosophy

A Deeply Personal Approach

No two transitions are alike. Every family brings different histories, different dynamics, and different definitions of "home." My role is not to impose a process, but to listen deeply, build trust, and then create a structure that fits your specific situation.

I believe that the best transitions happen when clarity comes first. Before we talk about timelines or moving trucks, we talk about what matters — what the person moving truly wants, what the family needs to feel at peace, and what success actually looks like for everyone involved.

I also believe in the power of preparation. Most of the stress families experience in these situations comes from decisions that are made too quickly, too late, or without enough information. My job is to create the space and structure for good decisions to happen.
